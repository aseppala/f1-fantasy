# F1 Fantasy 2026 - Team Strategies

Three teams, three distinct philosophies. Each team has its own identity and never copies another.

---

## Shared Principles (All Teams)

- **PPM (Points Per Million)** is the core evaluation metric for every pick
- **Monte Carlo simulator** validates manual analysis before locking in
- **Constructor-driver synergy:** pair constructors with their drivers when possible
- **Overtaking & position gains** are hidden PPM boosters — favor drivers who qualify low and race high
- **Price appreciation:** early-season picks should target underpriced drivers likely to rise
- **Transfer discipline:** 2 free per race, never burn extras unless swing >15 pts expected
- **Chip coordination:** never use the same chip on the same weekend across teams; spread them to maximize coverage

---

## Team 1: "Safe" (Conservative)

**Philosophy:** Don't bet against the dominant constructor. Ride the best team's form, fill with proven value picks. Consistent points, low variance.

### Construction Rules
- **Always include the constructor on best current form** (whoever topped the last race weekend)
- **1 premium driver from that dominant team** — pick the cheaper of the two if close in pace
- **1 premium driver from the 2nd-best team** for diversification
- **3 budget drivers** ($5-8M) with proven scoring records and overtaking upside
- **2nd constructor:** value midfield pick ($6-8M) that synergizes with a budget driver

### 2x Driver
The premium driver on the dominant team. High floor > high ceiling.

### Chip Strategy
- Deploy chips conservatively — only when clear data supports it
- **Limitless** on the sprint weekend with the most predictable pecking order
- **No Negative** for wet races or street circuits
- **Final Fix** kept as late-season insurance

### Risk Profile
Low variance. Aims for top-3 in the friends league most weeks. Rarely wins a single round outright, but accumulates steady points.

---

## Team 2: "Constructor Kings" (Constructor-Heavy)

**Philosophy:** Why pay $50M+ for two premium drivers when two premium constructors capture the scoring of FOUR top drivers? Stack the best constructors, fill drivers with budget overtakers.

### Construction Rules
- **2 premium constructors** from the top 3 teams (~$45-55M on constructors)
- **0-1 mid-price driver** ($12-16M) as 2x captain — ideally from a top-4 team not covered by constructors
- **4-5 budget drivers** ($5-8M) focused on overtaking and position gains
- **No premium drivers** ($20M+) — all top-team exposure comes through constructors
- If budget allows after 2 premium constructors, upgrade the cheapest driver slot

### 2x Driver
The mid-price driver with the highest ceiling. Accept more variance here since the constructors provide the floor.

### Chip Strategy
- **Limitless** is less impactful for this team (already has premium constructors) — consider saving it or using it to temporarily add premium drivers
- **x3 Boost** on the mid-price captain for maximum swing
- **Auto Pilot** is strong here — lets the system find the best scorer among many budget picks

### Risk Profile
Medium variance. Constructors provide a reliable scoring floor. Upside comes from budget drivers overperforming. Wins when top teams go 1-2 and midfield is chaotic.

---

## Team 3: "Ferrari Nuclear" (All-In Ferrari)

**Philosophy:** Concentrated bet on Ferrari. Both drivers, the constructor, and a 2x captain from the Scuderia. When Ferrari wins, this team obliterates the league. When they don't, it crashes and burns.

### Construction Rules
- **Both Ferrari drivers** (Leclerc + Hamilton) — always, no exceptions
- **Ferrari constructor** — always, no exceptions
- **2 budget drivers** ($5-8M) to fill remaining slots
- **1 budget constructor** ($5-7M) — cheapest viable option
- Triple Ferrari exposure is the identity of this team; never dilute it

### 2x Driver
**Leclerc** by default (higher qualifying ceiling, more likely to score pole + win). Switch to Hamilton only if Leclerc has a grid penalty or Hamilton clearly has better race pace that weekend.

### Chip Strategy
- **x3 Boost** on a weekend where Ferrari are clear favorites — stack x3 on Leclerc + 2x on Hamilton for maximum Ferrari explosion
- **No Negative** when Ferrari face high-risk circuits (street circuits, wet races) to protect against the double-DNF nightmare
- **Limitless** is the ANTI-chip for this team — it removes the Ferrari concentration that defines it. Only use if Ferrari are genuinely off-pace and you want one week of diversification.

### Risk Profile
Highest variance of all three teams. Scoring range each round is enormous (-55 to 215+). This team exists to win individual rounds by 50+ points. Accept that some weeks it will finish last in the league. Over a full season, it needs Ferrari to be genuine title contenders to pay off.

### Ferrari Health Check (Every Round)
Before locking in, ask:
1. Are both Ferrari drivers in the top 5 in practice? If not, consider Final Fix or No Negative.
2. Any reliability concerns? New PU components?
3. Is this a Ferrari-friendly circuit (power circuits, low degradation)?
4. What's the weather? Rain increases variance — could be good or bad for concentration.

## How the Three Teams Work Together

| Scenario | Team 1 (Safe) | Team 2 (Constructors) | Team 3 (Ferrari) |
|----------|--------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Mercedes dominates | Best performer | Good (if Merc C picked) | Worst performer |
| Ferrari 1-2 | Good | Good (if Ferrari C picked) | Best performer |
| Chaos/rain/DNFs | Steady | Steady (constructors absorb) | High risk |
| Midfield surprise | Decent | Best (budget drivers score) | Irrelevant |

The three teams are deliberately uncorrelated. On any given weekend, at least one should perform well regardless of what happens at the front.
