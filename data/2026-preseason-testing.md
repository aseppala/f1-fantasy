## Team-by-Team Assessment

### Tier 1: Title Contenders

**1. Mercedes**
- Best race simulation pace across all testing
- Russell's 1:36.5 long-run average was class of the field
- 400+ laps completed despite early mechanical issues (suspension, PU on days 1-2)
- Pre-race favorite heading into Australia
- Reluctant to copy Red Bull's aggressive downshifting (engine knock concerns)

**2. Ferrari**
- Fastest single-lap time (Leclerc 1:31.992)
- Innovative rotating rear wing and beam wing design
- Strong reliability and consistent pace across conditions
- Slightly behind Mercedes on race sim pace
- Both drivers (Leclerc + Hamilton) consistently fast

**3. McLaren**
- Competitive by week two of testing
- Norris admitted being "a little bit off" Ferrari & Mercedes race pace
- Andrea Stella acknowledged both Mercedes and Ferrari ran faster race sims
- NOT running definitive PU specification in testing (potential for improvement)
- Mercedes customer team

### Tier 2: Close Behind

**4. Red Bull**
- Set early benchmark but "eclipsed as testing progressed"
- Superior energy deployment on straights (Ford PU advantage)
- Able to deploy far more energy on straights than competitors over 10 consecutive laps
- Significant tire degradation concerns (lost 7s to Piastri over race distance)
- Verstappen fast but car has race pace questions

### Tier 3: Midfield

**5. Haas** - Well-balanced package. Solid midfield runner.
**6. Alpine** - Leading Mercedes customer team. Solid long-run pace.
**7. Racing Bulls** - Main midfield group. Lawson + Lindblad (rookie).
**8. Audi** - Showed improvement as testing progressed. Hulkenberg + Bortoleto.

### Tier 4: Struggling

**9. Williams** - Appeared heavy and aerodynamically limited.
**10. Cadillac** - New team, starting as backmarker. Bottas + Perez.
**11. Aston Martin** - WORST team in testing.
  - Only 206 laps completed (least of any team)
  - Both drivers under 100 laps each
  - Honda power unit reliability disasters
  - Arrived late to testing with significant issues

## Key Technical Notes

### Energy Management (New for 2026)
- ERS harvests 2x more energy per lap than 2025
- 50/50 split between electrical and ICE power
- Battery charging limitations = significant issue across the field
- Australia qualifying may feature "unwelcome energy management"
- FIA considering "super clipping" technology

### Engine Compression Ratio Controversy
- Mercedes engine compression ratio attracted FIA attention
- FIA compromise: controlled in hot AND cold conditions from June 2026
- From 2027: only controlled in operating conditions (130°C)

### Two-Class Grid
- Top 4 (Mercedes, Ferrari, McLaren, Red Bull) separated by tenths
- ~1 second gap to midfield teams
- Clear performance cliff between top 4 and rest
